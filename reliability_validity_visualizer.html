Clinical and research implication
Key relationship between reliability and validity
  • Reliability is necessary but not sufficient for validity. You can be reliably wrong.
  • You cannot be consistently valid without being reliable — a scattered instrument cannot hit the target on average.
  • ASHA evidence-based practice (CAA standard IV-F) requires evidence of both for any measure used in clinical decision-making.
