Decision
✓ What p actually means
✓The probability of observing data this extreme or more if H₀ were true — the red tail area above
✓A long-run frequency: if H₀ is true and we repeated the study many times, p × 100% of samples would produce a test statistic at least this extreme
✓A statement about the data given H₀, written P(data | H₀)
✗ What p does NOT mean
✗The probability that H₀ is true — P(H₀ | data) requires a prior (Bayesian framework)
✗The probability that the result is "due to chance"
✗A measure of effect size or clinical importance — a large n can make a trivial effect "significant"
✗A meaningful divide: p = 0.049 and p = 0.051 are essentially identical
